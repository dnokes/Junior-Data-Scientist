## Program goals

**Vision Statement**

Enhancing and promoting Family Medicine that excels in patient-centred care,
scholarly activity and leadership for healthier communities.

**Mission Statement**

_A Centre of scholarly excellence committed to developing exemplary family
physicians and future leaders in healthcare through clinical excellence,
professional education, administrative leadership and robust research._

We achieve our mission through the following key goals:

  * Providing the highest quality patient-centred care which includes continuity of care, comprehensive care and prevention for all patients including marginalized groups;
  * Teaching undergraduate medical students those aspects of medicine which family physicians are best suited to teach and ensuring all Undergraduates understand the value of Family Medicine;
  * Training and mentoring our postgraduate residents in Family Medicine to ensure they acquire the knowledge, skills and attitudes necessary to be confident and competent family physicians and leaders;
  * Teaching our graduate students (Masters and PhD) the academic and research skills necessary to advance the discipline of Family Medicine world-wide;
  * Promoting and conducting interdisciplinary research that is relevant to the advancement and practice of Family Medicine;
  * Providing supports and professional development opportunities to our staff, faculty and preceptors;
  * Being strong primary care advocates through provincial, national and international health policy development.

**Statement of Overall Goals of Residency Program**

The family medicine residency-training program at Western University aims to
graduate patient-centred and comprehensive family physicians, and develop
future leaders for the discipline of family medicine through a flexible
training program that is able to meet personal goals of our trainees and
accreditation standards. Our trainees are evaluated through a multi-method
strategy that includes regular reviews of clinical performance and direct
observations.

## Selection process goals

Successful applicants will have the following skills and attributes:

  * Committed to providing high quality patient-centered care which includes continuity of care, comprehensive care and preventative care for all patients;
  * Demonstrate a commitment to life-long learning and sharing of knowledge through education, teaching and mentorship;
  * Promoting and conducting research and scholarly activity that is relevant to the advancement and practice of Family Medicine;
  * Being strong primary care advocates through local, provincial, national and international health policy development;
  * Demonstrate and continue to develop robust leadership skills to enhance the practice of Family Medicine.

# Program Curriculum

This residency program is for 2 years.

Program length of training does not exceed the Royal College or College of
Family Physicians of Canada standard.

 **Features of our Windsor Program**

  * Training in urban and suburban community practices
  * Ample elective opportunities during and outside of family medicine block time with teaching faculty who have a wide variety of special interests
  * Experience interdisciplinary care while working with nurse practitioners, pharmacists, physiotherapists

The Windsor program has an integrated 2-year curriculum. There are electronic
medical resources for residents, including computer and internet access, and
standard medical resources in each core Family Medicine training site.

Windsor's training program affords residents ample one-on-one time with
preceptors and provides a positive resident/preceptor experience overall.
Family Medicine residents are in high demand in Windsor due to the limited
number of residents in other specialties.
